Anza Historic Trails

Helping hikers find relevant trail and historic information to plan their trips

View Prototype
  • ←Previous
  • Next→

Summary

Problem
Big part of the Anza territory has bad mobile coverage. Planning ahead is a must. Yet, the information on the website was hard to find or inaccurate. e.g. It has 3 tabs to use the map, each with different functions. It's hard to find trails or even discover the website can provide directions.

Proposal
To make the website responsive and rearrange the info available. It aims to manage people’s expectations of how accurate the information can be. The flow allows users to find addresses, amenities and historic journals for each trail. It provides links to needed information missing.

Role
UX researcher, designer and consultant:
•Interviewer, Research Insights, Personas
•Scenarios, Strategy + User flow design + IA, Sketches, Interface design
•Wireframes, Visual Design and presentation with Interaction Design instructions and next steps.

Context

ABOUT ANZA TRAILS & THE PROJECT
My employer wanted to propose their client a mobile app for people that wanted access info on the go. I had to design it and they would use it to convince the client. My employer was GreenInfo Network. Their client was the Anza Historic Trails - National Park Service.

Challenges
Anza Historic Trails can’t afford an app, they simply wanted a responsive version of the current website. I had to work with what was already there to reach a balance between client, employer, and user goals.

Results
GreenInfo Network proceeded to use my research and recommendations. Unfortunately, the project had to stop due to lack of funds.

Timeline
4 weeks part time - October/November 2014
